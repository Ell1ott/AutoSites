Our story

A kitchen
between two
oceans.

Atami opened on a rainy November evening in 2014, a single eight-seat counter on Vesterbrogade. The idea was simple: treat Nordic ingredients with the precision of an Edomae sushi-ya, and treat Japanese rituals with the warmth of a Danish kro.

Today, four restaurants later, the rice is still rinsed by hand. The fish still arrives whole from the boat. The pickles are still made on Tuesday mornings by Hiro and Mette. Nothing about that is changing.

01 · 素材

Ingredients first

North Atlantic fish, Bornholm rice vinegar, hand-pressed wasabi from Vejle.

02 · 仕事

Quiet craft

Twelve itamae trained between Tokyo, Stockholm and our Copenhagen counter.

03 · 場所

Sense of place

Interiors by Norm Architects — pale oak, deep moss, washi light.

04 · 親切

Generous service

No tipping. No rush. Tea refilled, sake poured, conversations kept low.
